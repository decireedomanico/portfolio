Business Analysis

Business Problem

An online retail business offers a wide variety of sports and outdoor products, including 20 product categories such as Kayaks, Ski Boots, Surfboards, Fitness Trackers, and Running Shoes. Despite having consistent product availability and active marketing campaigns, the company struggled with uneven product performance and fluctuating monthly sales contribution.

Goal

The goal of this analysis is to identify the main business problem and assess customer engagement, conversion rates, and product ratings using the dataset shared by the marketing team to determine areas that need improvement.

Solution

To improve performance, the marketing team should prioritize products with consistently high conversion rates such as Kayaks, Ski Boots, and Baseball Gloves, and launch seasonal promotions or personalized campaigns during peak months (e.g., January and September) to maximize demand. To address declining views and low interaction rates, they should experiment with more engaging content formats like interactive videos and user-generated content (UGC) while improving results by optimizing call-to-action (CTA) placement across social media and blog content, especially during historically low-engagement months (September to December). Lastly, to improve customer ratings, the business should implement a structured feedback loop where mixed and negative reviews are analyzed to identify common issues, followed by improvement actions and customer follow-ups to resolve concerns and encourage re-rating, aiming to raise average ratings closer to the 4.0 target.

Data Cleansing & Transformation (SQL)

To build the necessary data model for marketing analysis, key tables were extracted and cleansed from the relational database using SQL. The engagement data was processed with Python for sentiment analysis and integrated during the data modeling stage in Power BI.

DIM_Customers Table

SQL Transformation for Customer Dimension

SELECT
 c.CustomerID,
 c.CustomerName,
 c.Email,
 c.Gender,
 c.Age,
 -- GeographyID -- 
 g.Country,
 g.City
 FROM dbo.customers as c
  LEFT JOIN  dbo.geography as g
  ON c.GeographyID = g.GeographyID;

Dim_Products Table

SQL Transformation for Product Dimension

SELECT 
  ProductID, 
  ProductName, 
  -- Category, --
  Price, 
  CASE 
  WHEN Price < 50 THEN 'Low' WHEN Price BETWEEN 50 
  AND 200 THEN 'Medium' ELSE 'High' END AS 'PriceCategory' 
FROM 
  [PortfolioProject_MarketingAnalytics].[dbo].[products];

Fact_Customer_Reviews Table

SQL Transformation for Customer Reviews

SELECT 
    ReviewID, 
    CustomerID, 
    ProductID,
    ReviewDate,
    Rating,  
    REPLACE(ReviewText, '  ', ' ') AS ReviewText
FROM 
    dbo.customer_reviews;

Fact_Customer_Journey Table

SQL Transformation for Customer Journey

WITH CleanedJourney AS (
    SELECT
        JourneyID,
        CustomerID,
        ProductID,
        VisitDate,
        UPPER(Stage) AS Stage,
        Action,
        Duration,
        AVG(Duration) OVER (PARTITION BY VisitDate) AS avg_duration,
        ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
            PARTITION BY CustomerID, ProductID, VisitDate, UPPER(Stage), Action
            ORDER BY JourneyID
        ) AS row_num
    FROM dbo.customer_journey
)
SELECT
    JourneyID,
    CustomerID,
    ProductID,
    VisitDate,
    Stage,
    Action,
    COALESCE(Duration, avg_duration) AS Duration
FROM CleanedJourney
WHERE row_num = 1
ORDER BY JourneyID;
